Hello, I'm Mani!

I'm a self-taught AI/ML engineer with 4+ years of hands-on experience across the full LLM development stack. Currently the Founding Engineer at RecurX, and doing independent research in mechanistic interpretability — recently published at Zenodo.

My work spans writing CUDA kernels, training 700M-parameter models from scratch, alignment research (RLHF, DPO, GRPO), and publishing independent mechanistic interpretability work. I believe the most interesting engineering happens at the boundary where research meets production.

My research focuses on understanding and building efficient large language models, with three core directions:

Mechanistic Interpretability — Reverse-engineering circuits inside transformers and sparse MoE models to understand how they represent and process information, including publishing Grokking Beyond Addition, a circuit-level study of algebraic learning boundaries.
Efficient Inference — Building faster serving systems through speculative decoding, custom FlashAttention-2 CUDA kernels, and sparse mixture-of-experts architectures that achieve 2–2.4× throughput gains.
Multilingual & Offline AI — Designing fully offline voice pipelines like VAANI, a Hindi-first assistant with sub-800ms end-to-end latency on CPU and zero internet dependency.

FlashAttention-2 CUDA Kernel
Custom GPU attention engine · 2026

Tiled IO-aware FlashAttention-2 from scratch in CUDA C++, fusing QKV matmul, softmax, and weighted-sum into a single SRAM-resident kernel. Reduces HBM bandwidth from O(N²) to O(N). Pip-installable PyTorch C++ extension.

⚡ 2.1× throughput vs PyTorch baseline (A100, seqlen 4096)
